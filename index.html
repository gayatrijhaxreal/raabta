The Meaning of Raabta

One word. Countless relationships.

"Raabta" is a word from Urdu and Hindi meaning — connection, contact, bond. It is not merely linguistic; it expresses a deep and universal human emotion that transcends language and culture.

When two hearts meet, when one community stands by another in sorrow, when a stranger holds the hand of someone in need — that is Raabta.

रा
Raabta (noun)
Connection · Contact · Bond · Union

A word prevalent in Hindi, Urdu and Persian that captures the depth of human relationships. This is our name, and this is our purpose.

Our Journey

The Story of Raabta

2019

The Foundation Stone

A small group came together and dreamed of Raabta — a platform that would connect every section of society through service and solidarity.

2020

COVID Relief Campaign

During the pandemic, we delivered ration kits, medicines and moral support to thousands of families who had nowhere else to turn.

2021

Expanding Education

Free online and offline classes launched — over 500 children benefited from our learning programmes in underserved communities.

2023

National Recognition

Expanded to 8 states, reached 5,000+ families and built a network of 200 dedicated volunteers committed to the cause.

2026

A New Horizon

Raabta now aims higher — in every district, every village, every heart. The mission continues, stronger than ever before.
